# Detailed Analysis of Top 10 Webcomics

## 1. xkcd

**URL:** https://xkcd.com/

**Author:** Randall Munroe

**Publication History:**
- Started: September 2005
- Duration: 20+ years (still ongoing)
- Background: Randall Munroe graduated from Christopher Newport University in 2006 with a degree in physics and worked as a roboticist for NASA before creating xkcd full-time in late 2006

**Publication Frequency:** Three times per week (Monday, Wednesday, Friday)

**Panel Count:** Typically 1 panel (single-panel format, though some comics have multiple panels)

**Text Placement:** Alt-text appears below the graphic. Dialogue/labels typically appear within the graphic itself

**Typical Word Count:** ~40-50 words in alt-text, plus minimal text in the comic itself

**Color vs. Black-and-White:** Black-and-white line art (stick figures)

**Reading Level:** Adult/college-level; requires technical knowledge in science, mathematics, and programming for full appreciation. Sophisticated conceptual humor.

**Monetization:**
- Merchandise sales (primarily thousands of T-shirts per month)
- Book sales
- By 2008, xkcd financially supported Munroe "reasonably well" through merchandise alone
- No traditional advertising
- Licensed under Creative Commons

## 2. Questionable Content

**URL:** http://questionablecontent.net/

**Author:** Jeph Jacques

**Publication History:**
- Started: August 1, 2003
- Duration: 22+ years (still ongoing)
- Reached comic #5,743+ as of 2026
- Comic #5,000 milestone reached in March 2023

**Publication Frequency:**
- Initially: 2 days per week
- Strip #16: Increased to 3 days per week
- September 2004-present: 5 days per week (Monday-Friday) when Jacques quit his day job

**Panel Count:** Multi-panel format (typically 4-6 panels per strip)

**Text Placement:** Speech bubbles within the graphic panels

**Typical Word Count:** Substantial dialogue; described as "technically a novella" for some strips

**Color vs. Black-and-White:** Full color

**Reading Level:** Adult; slice-of-life humor with science fiction elements. Accessible language but mature themes.

**Monetization:**
- Patreon support
- Google AdSense ads
- Playwire advertising network
- PayPal donations
- Merchandise sales through Topatoco

---

## 3. Saturday Morning Breakfast Cereal (SMBC)

**URL:** https://www.smbc-comics.com/

**Author:** Zach Weinersmith

**Publication History:**
- Original version: January 28, 2002 - September 3, 2002
- Current version started: September 5, 2002
- Duration: 23+ years (still ongoing)

**Publication Frequency:** Daily (since approximately 2005 when Weinersmith went full-time)

**Panel Count:** Variable format - ranges from single panel to 10+ panels. No set format.

**Text Placement:** Speech bubbles and captions within panels, plus the signature "red button" bonus panel

**Typical Word Count:** Variable; gag-a-day format with dialogue-heavy strips

**Color vs. Black-and-White:** Full color

**Reading Level:** College/adult level; themes include science, philosophy, mathematics, religion, and existentialism. Sophisticated wordplay and conceptual humor.

**Monetization:**
- Patreon support
- SMBC merchandise store (Shopify)
- Display advertising
- Email newsletter
- As of 2012, received 250,000+ visitors per day

---

## 4. The Oatmeal

**URL:** http://theoatmeal.com/

**Author:** Matthew Inman

**Publication History:**
- Started: 2009
- Duration: 16+ years
- Attracted 5 million monthly readers before first birthday
- Note: Announced hiatus around 2019 for ~2 years

**Publication Frequency:** Irregular; Inman doesn't limit himself to a schedule. Creates content when inspired rather than on fixed days.

**Panel Count:** Variable - infographic-style comics with multiple panels and sections

**Text Placement:** Text integrated throughout graphics, both in speech bubbles and as explanatory captions

**Typical Word Count:** High - often 100-500+ words per comic due to infographic/educational format

**Color vs. Black-and-White:** Full color with distinctive illustration style

**Reading Level:** General audience; accessible humor mixed with educational content. Wide appeal.

**Monetization:**
- Merchandise sales: $400,000/year (twice as much as advertising)
- Display advertising: ~$200,000/year
- Book deals
- Total revenue in 2012: $500,000/year
- Initial donation button received strong response
- Later expanded to comprehensive merchandise line

## 5. Penny Arcade

**URL:** https://www.penny-arcade.com/comic

**Authors:** Mike Krahulik (artist) and Jerry Holkins (writer)

**Publication History:**
- Started: November 18, 1998
- Duration: 27+ years (still ongoing)
- By 2000, popular enough for creators to quit day jobs
- By 2010, had 3.5 million readers

**Publication Frequency:** Three times per week (Monday, Wednesday, Friday)

**Panel Count:** Typically 3-4 panels in traditional comic strip format

**Text Placement:** Speech bubbles within panels, occasional narrative text

**Typical Word Count:** ~50-100 words per strip

**Color vs. Black-and-White:** Full color

**Reading Level:** Adult; focuses on video game culture and gaming industry. Requires familiarity with gaming to fully appreciate references.

**Monetization:**
- Originally: Donation-supported
- After hiring business manager Robert Khoo: Shifted to advertising and merchandise revenue
- Created PAX (Penny Arcade Expo) gaming conventions
- Child's Play charity
- Among the first webcomic creators successful enough to make a full living from their work

---

## 6. Cyanide & Happiness

**URL:** https://explosm.net/

**Authors:** Kris Wilson, Rob DenBleyker, Matt Melvin (left 2014), Dave McElfatrick (left 2025), plus contributing artists

**Publication History:**
- Explosm.net launched: December 9, 2004
- Cyanide & Happiness first appeared: January 26, 2005
- Duration: 20+ years (still ongoing)
- By 2012: Receiving over 1 million views per day

**Publication Frequency:** Daily

**Panel Count:** Variable - typically 3-4 panels, but ranges from single panel to longer formats

**Text Placement:** Speech bubbles and captions within panels

**Typical Word Count:** ~20-40 words per strip (minimalist dialogue)

**Color vs. Black-and-White:** Simple color (typically using basic colors on white background)

**Reading Level:** Adult; dark humor with mature themes. Simple vocabulary but sophisticated comedic concepts.

**Monetization:**
- Kickstarter campaigns (2013 campaign raised $770,309 for animated series)
- Patreon (to fund projects after YouTube demonetization issues)
- Five published books
- Merchandise
- Craft beer
- YouTube channel (ExplosmEntertainment) for animated shorts

---

## 7. Lore Olympus

**URL:** https://www.webtoons.com/en/romance/lore-olympus/

**Author:** Rachel Smythe

**Publication History:**
- Originally on Tumblr: 2017
- Webtoon Discover: April 2017
- Relaunched on Webtoon Canvas: March 4, 2018
- Concluded: June 1, 2024
- Duration: ~7 years (complete)
- Total: 3 seasons, 280 episodes
- By 2019: 299+ million views (most viewed webcomic on Webtoon)

**Publication Frequency:** Weekly (every Sunday) until completion

**Panel Count:** Vertical scroll format (webtoon) - continuous panels designed for mobile reading

**Text Placement:** Speech bubbles within the scrolling panels

**Typical Word Count:** Variable; substantial dialogue in narrative arcs

**Color vs. Black-and-White:** Full color with distinctive watercolor-style art. Each god is a different color (Persephone in bright pink, Hades in bold blue). Pops of color on ink and blue backgrounds.

**Reading Level:** Young Adult/Adult; retelling of Greek mythology with mature themes (trauma, healing, relationships)

**Monetization:**
- Webtoon platform (WEBTOON monetization program)
- Published print volumes through Del Rey Books
- Won two Eisner Awards, two Harvey Awards, and two Ringo Awards
- Massive fanbase and merchandise opportunities

## 8. Batman: Wayne Family Adventures

**URL:** https://www.webtoons.com/en/slice-of-life/batman-wayne-family-adventures/

**Authors:**
- Writer: CRC Payne
- Lead Artist (Inks): StarBite
- Storyboards: Maria Li
- Backgrounds: Lan Ma
- Lettering: Kielamel Sibal
- Coloring: C.M. Cameron, Camille Cruz, Jean Kim

**Publication History:**
- Started: September 8, 2021 (Batman Day 2021)
- Duration: 4+ years (still ongoing)
- First DC Comics/Webtoon collaboration
- Five volumes published

**Publication Frequency:** Weekly (every Thursday - New Comic Book Day)

**Panel Count:** Vertical scroll webtoon format with continuous panels

**Text Placement:** Speech bubbles and sound effects within panels

**Typical Word Count:** ~100-150 words per episode

**Color vs. Black-and-White:** Full color from launch

**Reading Level:** General audience/Young Adult; family-friendly slice-of-life comedy featuring the Batfamily. Accessible to both comics fans and newcomers.

**Monetization:**
- Webtoon platform revenue
- DC Comics partnership
- Among the most popular DC webtoon properties
- Print volumes available
- Part of larger DC/Webtoon initiative

## 9. The Order of the Stick

**URL:** https://www.giantitp.com/comics/oots.html

**Author:** Rich Burlew

**Publication History:**
- Started: September 29, 2003
- Duration: 22+ years (still ongoing)
- Over 1,300 episodes as of September 2025

**Publication Frequency:**
- Initially: Twice weekly (Mondays and Thursdays)
- After first book's success: Three times per week
- Since 2007: Irregular schedule due to author's health concerns
- Current: Random schedule depending on author's ability to work

**Panel Count:** 1-4 pages per episode (typically multiple panels per page)

**Text Placement:** Speech bubbles within panels

**Typical Word Count:** Variable; dialogue-heavy strips with 100-300+ words

**Color vs. Black-and-White:** Full color

**Reading Level:** Adult; requires familiarity with Dungeons & Dragons and tabletop RPG mechanics. Sophisticated plot with literary references and complex storylines.

**Monetization:**
- Pre-orders of first compilation book (December 2004) allowed Burlew to quit his day job
- Multiple print compilations
- Kickstarter campaign (January 2012): Raised $1,254,120 - the most funded creative work on Kickstarter at that time
- Patreon for ongoing support
- Merchandise

## 10. Dinosaur Comics

**URL:** https://qwantz.com/

**Author:** Ryan North

**Publication History:**
- Started: August 1, 2003
- Duration: 22+ years (still ongoing)
- Created during North's last year of undergraduate degree
- The URL qwantz.com was given for a school assignment

**Publication Frequency:** Daily (continues to update daily as of 2025)

**Panel Count:** Exactly 6 panels (same panels every single day - constrained comic format)

**Text Placement:** Speech bubbles positioned above/beside dinosaur characters. Traditional comic placement.

**Typical Word Count:** ~100-120 words per strip

**Color vs. Black-and-White:** Black-and-white (uses the same clipart dinosaur images for every strip)

**Reading Level:** Adult; philosophical and absurdist humor. Accessible language but sophisticated conceptual comedy.

**Monetization:**
- Merchandise (T-shirts, posters, plush toys) through TopatoCo
- Patreon (supporters get early access to strips and bonus content)
- Print publication in some newspapers and magazines
- Book sales (multiple published collections)
- North states that making Dinosaur Comics is his full-time job
- Mailing list and community engagement

## Common Monetization Strategies

All top 10 webcomics employ multiple revenue streams:

1. **Merchandise** - T-shirts, posters, books (all 10 comics)
2. **Patreon/Subscriptions** - Recurring supporter funding (8 of 10)
3. **Advertising** - Display ads (5 of 10)
4. **Book Sales** - Print compilations (all 10 comics)
5. **Crowdfunding** - Kickstarter campaigns (at least 2)
6. **Platform Revenue** - Webtoon platform monetization (2 of 10)

## Notable Trends

- **Update Frequency:** Daily or 3-5x/week most common
- **Color:** 8 of 10 are full color; only 2 remain black-and-white
- **Longevity:** Most have been running 15-27 years
- **Reading Level:** Predominantly adult audience
- **Text Placement:** All use traditional speech bubbles or captions within graphics
- **Panels:** Highly variable, from 1 panel to continuous vertical scroll formats
- **Word Count:** Ranges from 20-300+ words per strip
